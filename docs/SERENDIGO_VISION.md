# PART 1 — Sri Lanka as the Content

Before any screen or database model, we must understand what Sri Lanka actually *is* — because the island itself is the product. Most travel apps treat destinations as data. We treat Sri Lanka as the world's most underrated adventure.

---

## 1.1 Sri Lanka at a Glance (Why This Place Is Special)

Sri Lanka is the size of Ireland. In it, you have:
- **6 UNESCO World Heritage Sites** (more per square kilometre than almost anywhere)
- **The world's largest gathering of Asian elephants** (Minneriya National Park)
- **Blue whales** 1 km off the southern coast — the closest whale watching on earth
- **The world's most scenic train journey** (Kandy to Ella, 6 hours of mountain rainforest)
- **Wild leopards** at higher density than most African reserves (Yala)
- **Ancient cities** that predate Rome — Anuradhapura, Polonnaruwa
- **9 surf breaks** that between them catch swell all year
- **Ayurveda** that has been practised here for 3,000 years
- **Ceylon tea** — 70% of the world drinks it
- **A cuisine** that uses spices so well that most visitors eat better here than anywhere they've been

And the entire thing is a 6-hour drive end to end. You don't have to choose — you can have all of it.

The app must reflect this richness. A travel app for Sri Lanka that doesn't help you eat well, surf well, find wild elephants, or sit with a monk for an hour is not a travel app for Sri Lanka.

## 1.2 The Five Experience Worlds

Everything in SerendiGO — every story arc, every chapter, every XP category — is organized around five worlds that map to how travellers actually think and talk about their trips.

### World 1: TASTE
*Sri Lanka's food is one of the world's great undiscovered cuisines*

**The Food Story (what the app should teach):**
Sri Lankan food is not Indian food. It's coconut-forward, tamarind-sour, dried-fish-funky, and chilli-hot in ways that build slowly. It's the food of a tropical island that has been trading spices for 2,000 years. Every region has its own version. A meal in Jaffna tastes nothing like a meal in Galle.

**Content Chapters (examples):**
- **Hoppers at Dawn** — Find a hopper kadé and eat a well hopper with pol sambol before 8am
- **The Kottu Rhythm** — Follow the metallic clang of kottu being made, eat where the crowds are
- **String Hoppers and Coconut Milk** — The Sunday breakfast ritual in a local home
- **Rice and Curry, the Right Way** — Sit on the floor, eat with your right hand, use the gravy properly
- **A Cup of Real Ceylon Tea** — Visit a factory, understand grades (BOP, FBOP, Pekoe), taste the difference
- **King Coconut (Thambili)** — The orange coconut unique to Sri Lanka, every 500m on the roadside
- **Watalappan** — The coconut custard dessert that came from the Malay community, find the best in Colombo
- **The Jaffna Crab** — Mud crab in a black curry, the most talked-about dish in the north
- **Lamprais** — Dutch-Burgher rice + curry + cutlet + blachan wrapped in banana leaf and steamed
- **Galle Face Green at Sunset** — Street food strip: isso vadi, chilli cuttlefish, sweetcorn
- **The Muslim Biryani** — Trincomalee and Arugam Bay have Sri Lanka's finest rice dishes
- **The Spice Garden Walk** — Matale district, smell cinnamon on the bark before it's processed

**Food XP:** Added as a fifth XP category: `tasteXP`

**Food Arcs (full story arcs):**
- *The Colombo Street Food Circuit* (Urban, 1 day)
- *Jaffna: Flavours of the North* (Regional, 2–3 days)
- *Tea Country Table* (Hill Country, 2 days)
- *The Southern Seafood Coast* (Galle → Tangalle, 3 days)
- *A Tamil Kitchen* (Cooking class arc — find a home, cook, eat)
- *Spice Road* (Matale → Kandy, following spice trade routes)

### World 2: WILD
*Sri Lanka has some of the densest wildlife on earth, and almost no one knows it*

**The Wildlife Story:**
Most people come to Sri Lanka for the culture and leave having had an unexpected wildlife obsession. The island has leopards, elephants, sloth bears, sambar deer, crocodiles, 250 endemic bird species, blue whales, sperm whales, spinner dolphins, five species of sea turtle, and more. Most of it is accessible without a private jet.

**Content Chapters (examples):**
- **The Elephant Gathering** — Minneriya National Park during dry season (July–October): up to 300 elephants at one watering hole
- **Spot a Leopard at Yala** — Go at dawn, wait at the water holes
- **Blue Whale at Mirissa** — 1 km offshore, the largest animal on earth
- **Baby Elephants at Udawalawa** — Elephant Transit Home: feeding times, ethical tourism done right
- **Nesting Sea Turtles at Rekawa** — Night visit (November–February), no flash photography
- **Endemic Bird Morning** — 14 endemic species, birding walk at dawn
- **Crocodile Kayaking** — Bentota River mangroves, monitor lizards and saltwater crocs
- **The Night Safari** — Wilpattu by jeep after dark, spotlight sighting
- **Whale Shark at Trinco** — Pigeon Island snorkeling
- **Turtle Hatchery Visit** — Kosgoda, hold a hatchling, understand conservation trade-offs

**Wildlife Arcs:**
- *The Elephant Circuit* (Minneriya → Udawalawa, 3 days)
- *Yala: Land of the Leopard* (Yala NP, 2 days)
- *The Southern Ocean Giants* (Mirissa whale watching, 1 day)
- *Wings Over Sri Lanka* (Endemic birding, hill country, 2 days)
- *The Marine Trail* (Trinco → Mirissa, diving + whales + turtles)

**Wild XP:** Mapped to `adventureXP` (shared with physical adventure activities)

### World 3: MOVE
*Sri Lanka rewards people who move through it — by train, by board, by foot, by boat*

**The Movement Story:**
The island has the world's most scenic railway, nine surf breaks with year-round swell rotating between east and west coasts, white-water rivers, rainforest treks, ancient staircases, and an 870km coastal road. The best travellers in Sri Lanka don't sit still.

**Content Chapters (examples):**
- **The Kandy–Ella Train** — Book the right seats (right side, facing direction of travel), don't miss Demodara Nine Arch Bridge
- **First Surf at Weligama** — Beginner-friendly long beach, lessons available, reef-free
- **Arugam Bay Dawn Patrol** — The main point break, arrive before the crowds, October surfable by pros
- **White Water Rafting at Kitulgala** — Class 3–4, the river from *The Bridge on the River Kwai* was filmed here
- **Adam's Peak Night Climb** — Start at 2am, summit at sunrise, pilgrimage route
- **World's End at Horton Plains** — 5am start, before the mist, sheer 870m drop
- **Knuckles Range Trek** — Off-grid, leech socks required, waterfalls and cloud forest
- **Sinharaja Rainforest** — UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, mixed-species bird flocks at dawn
- **Ella Rock** — No guide needed, follow the train tracks from town, 3-hour return
- **Coastal Cycle** — Colombo → Galle by bicycle: flat coast road, 120km, 2 days
- **Kayaking the Madu River** — Mangrove tunnels and cinnamon island visit

**Move Arcs:**
- *The Great Train Journey* (Colombo → Kandy → Ella, 4 days, all scenic routes)
- *Surf Sri Lanka* (Weligama → Arugam Bay following the season)
- *The Sacred Climbs* (Adam's Peak + Sigiriya + Pidurutalagala + Ella Rock)
- *Through the Rainforest* (Knuckles + Sinharaja, 4 days)
- *The Southern Cycle* (Colombo → Galle coastal road)

**Move XP:** Mapped to `actionXP`

### World 4: ROOTS
*Sri Lanka is ancient. The stories are encoded in stone, ritual, dance, and clay*

**The Roots Story:**
Sri Lanka's recorded history goes back 2,600 years. The ancient cities of the North Central Province were capitals of kingdoms that built reservoirs the size of lakes, dagobas the size of pyramids, and hospitals with surgical instruments that wouldn't look out of place in a modern theatre. The culture is still alive — monks still chant the same pali texts, kovils still explode with colour at festival time, craftspeople still carve masks the same way their ancestors did.

**Content Chapters (examples):**
- **Climb Sigiriya at Sunrise** — The 5th century rock fortress, frescoes 200m above the jungle
- **Walk Polonnaruwa by Bicycle** — The medieval capital, ruins spread across 10km
- **The Sacred Tooth** — Temple of the Tooth Relic, Kandy, dawn puja, drums and incense
- **A Buddhist Poya Night** — Full moon: temples open late, white-clad devotees, no alcohol on the island
- **The Perahera** — Kandy Esala Perahera (July/August): elephants, fire dancers, drummers, 10 nights
- **Devil Dance in Ambalangoda** — A Kolam mask dance performance, understand the ancient tradition
- **Carve a Mask** — Workshop with a traditional mask maker, Ambalangoda
- **Galle Fort at Dusk** — Dutch fort from 1663, still inhabited, best golden hour in Sri Lanka
- **Batik Workshop in Kandy** — Make your own piece of fabric with wax and dye
- **The Hindu Kovil at Nallur** — Jaffna's most important kovil, August festival is extraordinary
- **Dambulla Cave Temple** — 153 statues, 2,100 sq metres of paintings, 2,000 years of worship
- **Anuradhapura by Bicycle** — Ancient capital, stupas you can circumambulate, monkeys and pilgrims together
- **A Night at a Village Temple** — Not a tourist temple, a working village temple at puja time

**Roots Arcs:**
- *The Ancient Kingdom Route* (Anuradhapura → Polonnaruwa → Sigiriya, 4 days)
- *Kandyan Soul* (Kandy cultural circle, 2 days)
- *The Dutch South* (Galle Fort and colonial trail, 1–2 days)
- *The Northern Kovils* (Jaffna Hindu temples, 2 days)
- *Living Crafts* (Mask making, batik, pottery — artisan circuit)

**Roots XP:** Mapped to `cultureXP`

### World 5: RESTORE
*Sri Lanka is one of the world's great wellness destinations — and it invented it*

**The Restore Story:**
Ayurveda was developed in Sri Lanka and India simultaneously over 3,000 years ago. The country has genuine practitioners, not the watered-down spa version sold to tourists. Buddhist meditation retreats are some of the most serious in Asia. The hill country air at altitude is genuinely different. And simply sitting with a cup of tea watching the mist roll over a tea estate does something to you that a gym never will.

**Content Chapters (examples):**
- **Your First Ayurveda Consultation** — A real practitioner diagnoses your Dosha, prescribes treatment
- **The Abhyanga Oil Massage** — Traditional full-body oil treatment, therapeutic not relaxation
- **Meditation at a Forest Monastery** — Nissarana Vanaya, Neerella, or a forest monastery near Kandy: sit, breathe, be silent
- **Sunrise Yoga on the Rooftop** — A structured session overlooking tea estates or beach
- **Wild Swimming at Diyaluma** — Sri Lanka's second-highest waterfall, a natural pool
- **The Silence Day** — One day at a meditation centre, phone off, observe Noble Silence
- **Herbal Bath Ritual** — Soaking in an herbal infusion specific to your body type
- **Tea Estate Walk at Dawn** — Just walking slowly, breathing the altitude, watching pluckers work
- **Watching the Sunset from Mirissa Hill** — 30-minute climb, 360-degree ocean view, no crowds
- **A Night with No Electricity** — An eco-lodge in Knuckles or Sinharaja, fireflies, the real sky

**Restore Arcs:**
- *The Ayurveda Journey* (Colombo → Beruwala/Galle, 5–7 days for proper treatment)
- *Mindful Hill Country* (Nuwara Eliya → Kandy, slow, intentional)
- *The Beach Restoration* (South Coast, 3–4 days of pure ocean time)
- *Forest Silence* (Sinharaja / Knuckles meditation and nature retreat)

## 1.3 The Seasonal Intelligence System

This is one of the most useful features for a Sri Lanka travel app and almost nothing else offers it well. Sri Lanka's geography means the monsoon hits the west coast while the east coast is sunny, and vice versa. Activities are strongly seasonal. The app should be the traveller's seasonal advisor.

**The Two Monsoons:**
- **South-West Monsoon** (May–September): West coast and Hill Country wet. East coast (Arugam Bay, Trinco) is perfect.
- **North-East Monsoon** (November–February): East coast wet. West and South coast (Galle, Mirissa, Weligama) are perfect.

**Seasonal Calendar (built into every Arc and Chapter):**

| Activity | Best Season | Why |
|----------|-------------|-----|
| Surfing — Weligama, Hikkaduwa | November–April | SW monsoon gone, consistent swell |
| Surfing — Arugam Bay | May–October | Offshore winds, best point break |
| Whale watching — Mirissa | November–April | Calm seas, blue whales |
| Whale watching — Trincomalee | May–September | Sperm whales, different species |
| Yala leopards | Feb–July | Dry season, low vegetation, water holes |
| Elephant Gathering — Minneriya | August–October | Drought drives 200+ elephants to the tank |
| Adam's Peak climb | December–May | Pilgrimage season, path lit and clear |
| Horton Plains — World's End | Early morning, Dec–April | Before the mist arrives |
| Sea turtle nesting — Rekawa | November–February | Green and leatherback nesting season |
| Knuckles trekking | January–April | Dry, clear, leeches manageable |
| Sinharaja Rainforest | January–April, August | Less rain, bird activity |
| Kandy Esala Perahera | July–August | 10-night procession, pre-book |
| Sinhala/Tamil New Year | April 13–14 | Biggest cultural festival |
| Vesak Festival | May (full moon) | Buddhist festival, lanterns everywhere |
| Nallur Kovil Festival | August | Hindu festival, Jaffna |
| Colombo — year round | Always | Urban, manageable any time |

**How this works in the app:**
- Every Arc has `seasonStart` and `seasonEnd` fields
- In-season arcs show a green "Active Now" badge on the map icon
- Out-of-season arcs show "Best: Nov–Apr" with a greyed icon
- Today tab shows: "Whale watching season opens in 3 weeks — save Mirissa"
- Push notification when a bookmarked arc comes into season
- The AI trip planner uses season data when routing the itinerary

# PART 2 — The Traveller, Deeply Understood

## 2.1 Who Travels to Sri Lanka

**Primary target — The Explorer (22–35):** Travels 2–4 weeks. Budget $40–80/day. Hostels and mid-range guesthouses. Has done Southeast Asia. Sri Lanka feels fresh. They want to discover, not be toured. Will use an app daily if it genuinely finds things they'd have missed. They share heavily. Their social content is free marketing.

**Primary target — The Experience Seeker (28–45):** Couple or small group. 10–14 days. $100–200/day. They want 2 "wow" moments per day. They'll pay for a great local guide if they trust them. They currently book experiences through generic platforms that pay local operators poorly. We replace those platforms.

**Secondary — The Digital Nomad (25–40):** 1–3 months. Wants to go deep. These become "Local Author" contributors — they know a region better than most tourists ever will.

**Future Phase 3 — Domestic Traveller:** Sri Lankans exploring their own island. Different UX, LKR pricing, Sinhala UI option. Enormous market. Don't split focus in Phase 1.

# PART 3 — Business Model

---

## 3.1 Revenue Streams (Phased)

### Stream 1: Marketplace Commission — 12% — Phase 1
Local guides, surf lessons, cooking classes, wildlife safaris, diving, yoga, Ayurveda, tuk-tuk day tours, boat trips, train ticket facilitation, cooking classes, cultural workshops.

Why 12%? Viator and GetYourGuide charge operators 20–30%. Airbnb Experiences charges 20%. Starting at 12% attracts the best local operators fast. Volume compensates. Raise to 15% after achieving density.

**Year 1 target:** 80 active vendors × $500/month avg GMV × 12% = ~$4,800/month commission, growing to $20,000+/month.

### Stream 2: Serendib Partner Program — Phase 1
Local restaurants, tea shops, surf schools, spice farms, boutique guesthouses.

| Tier | Price | What they get |
|------|-------|---------------|
| Listed (Free) | $0 | Profile, searchable |
| Partner | $30/month | Featured, map pin, recommendations |
| Premium | $80/month | Flash Deals, priority map, monthly analytics |

**Flash Deals:** Visible only when user is within 1.5km AND has 200+ Serendipity Coins. 1-hour validity. Creates urgency and drives real foot traffic.

### Stream 3: Explorer+ Subscription — Phase 2

| Plan | Price | Who buys |
|------|-------|----------|
| Day Pass | $1.99 | Budget travellers wanting offline for one day |
| Week Pass | $4.99 | Most popular — matches a typical trip |
| Month | $9.99 | Digital nomads |

**Included in Explorer+:**
- Offline arc downloads (maps + all content)
- Insider Arcs (Local Author content, not free)
- Seasonal alerts for bookmarked arcs
- Passport PDF export at full resolution
- Flash Deals without coin requirement
- Fair Price Negotiation Guide (detailed LKR price ranges by region)

### Stream 4: Tourism Board Partnership — Phase 2
SLTDA (Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority) and provincial councils.

Partnership model: Government funds story arc production in underpromoted provinces (North, East, Uva, Sabaragamuwa). SerendiGO gets paid per arc + monthly maintenance retainer. Co-branded "Official Sri Lanka Tourism" content built to our quality standard.

Realistic deal: $30,000–$60,000 for a province campaign.

### Stream 5: Sponsored Arcs (Capped) — Phase 3
A hotel group, airline, or sustainable brand sponsors one premium arc per quarter. Clearly labeled. Hard cap: 2 sponsored arcs per 10 organic arcs. Protects trust.

Price: $8,000–$20,000 per arc.

### Stream 6: Anonymised Travel Insights — Phase 3
Aggregated, opt-in travel pattern data. Where do travellers actually go vs. what they say? Which provinces are undervisited? What arcs drive marketplace bookings? Sold to: hotel chains planning locations, transport operators, provincial development boards.

# PART 4 — The Local Community Model

---

## 4.1 The Three Principles

1. **Every local earns, not just benefits** — Every feature involving a local person has a direct income mechanism.
2. **Authenticity is the product** — The thing travellers pay for is genuine local experience. No big chains dominating.
3. **The app is infrastructure, not a boss** — Locals own their listings, set prices, manage schedules. We provide the platform and the trust layer.

---

## 4.2 Local Guide Certification

**Certified Local (Free to apply):**
- English proficiency test (conversational, practical)
- Regional knowledge quiz
- NIC identity verification
- 3 character references
- Earns: SerendiGO Certified badge, listed on marketplace, 12% commission (vs no listing)

**Senior Certified (Requires 6 months + 20 reviews):**
- Red Cross first aid certificate (app sponsors the training)
- Cultural sensitivity module (built in-app, 2 hours, created with university partners)
- Earns: Senior Guide badge, priority placement, ability to mentor new guides

**Guide earning model:**
- Set own price
- Paid within 48 hours of completion (not 30 days like most platforms)
- Earnings dashboard in-app
- Seasonal booking calendar
- Review responses panel

---

## 4.3 Local Author Program

Anyone can submit a story arc for their hometown.

**How it works:**
1. Apply via app ("I want to share my place")
2. Submit draft: 3–7 chapter locations, descriptions, personal intro as narrator, Before You Go info
3. Editorial review: quality, authenticity, accuracy
4. Published with attribution: *"Written by Chaminda, who has lived in Jaffna for 50 years"*
5. Author earns 5% of marketplace bookings generated from their arc
6. Top-rated authors featured in "Local Voices" section of Today tab

**What this creates:** Content money cannot buy. Competitive moat. Permanent goodwill.

---

## 4.4 Small Business Discovery Engine

Priority categories for listing:
- Home kitchens (women cooking traditional food for travellers)
- Village craftspeople (mask making, weaving, pottery, gem cutting)
- Family-run guesthouses with character
- Small-batch tea producers (boutique estate, not Dilmah)
- Organic spice farms in Matale
- Traditional practitioners (Ayurveda, Siddha medicine)

**"Meet the Maker" arc type:** Curated arcs following artisans in a region. "The Craft Trail of Sabaragamuwa." Each chapter is meeting a different maker. One-tap order from their workshop if they sell products.

---

## 4.5 The Serendib Community Fund

**2% of all marketplace revenue → Community Fund**

Fund balance visible on the app. Each quarter: 3–4 nominated projects. Users vote (one vote, requires at least 1 completed chapter). Top project receives the funding that quarter. Progress updates shown in-app as a story.

**Example projects:**
- Restore a wall painting at a temple featured in an arc
- Fund a wildlife ranger's salary for 6 months in a depleted national park
- Plant 500 trees along the Sinharaja buffer zone where an arc passes
- Supply a village school library near the Kandy arc's end point

This is the most press-worthy feature. "Travel app funds Sri Lanka village school through tourist votes" — that headline writes itself.

---

## 4.6 Fair Price Guide

A crowdsourced, admin-moderated price reference for everything travellers get overcharged for.

Shown on every relevant screen. Not prescriptive — informational. Updated by community, flagged if outdated.

Examples:
- Tuk-tuk: Colombo Fort → Pettah = LKR 250–400 (2025)
- Temple entry: Tooth Relic, Kandy = LKR 3,000 foreigners
- Surf lesson: Weligama, 2 hours = USD 18–30
- Cooking class: Home kitchen, 3 hours = USD 20–40
- Private guide: Full day = USD 50–80
- Whale watching: Mirissa boat = USD 35–55

This single feature builds more trust than any marketing.

---

## 4.7 Wildlife & Conservation Integration

Partner with: DWNC, Elephant Transit Home, Sea Turtle Protection Society, Rainforest Protectors of Sri Lanka.

When a traveller completes a Wildlife arc:
- 50 Serendipity Coins convert to a conservation contribution
- Visualised: "Your visit funded 2 nesting turtle monitoring nights"
- Partners verify and display the impact

This resolves the traveller's guilt about "just taking" from wildlife destinations and gives them a verified positive impact story to share.

## 5.1 Design Philosophy

**One sentence:** Feel like a beautifully illustrated field journal that happens to be a mobile app.

The visual language draws from three sources:
1. **Sri Lankan batik** — organic, warm, place-specific patterns in the province map borders
2. **Vintage National Geographic cartography** — illustrated maps, hand-lettered place names, warm parchment tones
3. **Contemporary mobile design** — clean, gesture-first, no visual clutter

Together: distinctive, culturally rooted, completely intuitive.

## 5.5 Motion Design

**Stamp Animation (the emotional peak):**
- Stamp presses down: scale 1.0 → 0.92 (15ms, spring)
- Hold: 120ms
- Release: 0.92 → 1.0 (200ms, spring overshoot to 1.04, settle)
- Ink spread: radial fill from center, 300ms ease-out, province colour
- Haptic: heavy impact on press, medium impact on release
- Province name fades in below stamp: 400ms delay

**Coin Rain (earning reward):**
- 8–14 coin icons emit from capture button at different angles and velocities
- Physics: arc upward then fall with gravity toward coin counter in header
- Counter increments with spring tick per coin arrival
- Haptic: light impact per coin arrival, medium on counter settle
- Total duration: 1.2 seconds

**Province Glow (entering new province):**
- Province polygon on map: amber glow pulses twice (scale 1.0 → 1.02 → 1.0)
- Banner slides from top: "You've entered Central Province · 3 arcs available"
- If first time in this province: full stamp animation plays inline
- Haptic: heavy impact

**Arc Complete + Hidden Gem Reveal:**
- Map: the hidden gem location materialises — fades in with a golden pulse
- Message from local author: slides up from bottom, DM Serif, italic
- Haptic: notification pattern

**The Island Map Pan/Zoom:**
- Gesture Handler + Reanimated 4 worklets
- Paper parallax: Skia noise texture layer moves at 0.8× the map speed — feels physical
- Province labels fade in at zoom level >2
- Arc icons scale slightly (1.0 → 1.12) on zoom to maintain visual hierarchy
- Target: 60fps on mid-range Android (Redmi Note 12 class device)

**Page Transitions:**
- Arc detail: shared element — arc card image expands to fill header (Reanimated shared value)
- Passport: book-open perspective animation (2D transform)
- Capture screen: circular reveal expanding from FAB position
- Everything else: spring slide, no linear easing

## 6.3 Backend Stack

**Runtime: Bun**
Bun v1.3 is production-ready (already installed on this machine). 3–5× faster than Node.js for TypeScript execution. Built-in test runner. Built-in package manager (faster installs). Compatible with almost all Node.js packages. For a fresh build in 2026, there's no reason to start on Node.js.

**Framework: Hono**
Ultra-lightweight (12kb), TypeScript-first, runs on Bun natively. 3× faster than Express in benchmarks. Bun-native. Clean middleware pattern. If you ever want to move to Cloudflare Workers (edge, zero cold start), Hono runs there without changes. This is the Express replacement for 2026.

**ORM: Drizzle**
Replaces Prisma. No code generation step. SQL-like queries that are fully type-safe. Lighter bundle, faster queries, better TypeScript inference. Schema defined in TypeScript files. Drizzle Studio replaces Prisma Studio. Migration system is as good.

```
Backend Stack:
  Runtime:    Bun 1.3+
  Framework:  Hono
  ORM:        Drizzle ORM
  Database:   PostgreSQL 16 + PostGIS extension
  Cache:      Redis 7 (Upstash managed, or self-hosted)
  Job Queue:  BullMQ (on Redis)
  Auth:       Better Auth (Google OAuth + Apple Sign-In + JWT)
  Payments:   Stripe + PayHere
  Images:     Cloudflare R2 + Cloudflare Images
  AI:         OpenAI GPT-4o (trip planner) + Whisper (audio phrases)
  Email:      Resend
  Realtime:   Ably (Phase 2)
  Monitoring: Sentry + Axiom (logs)
  Analytics:  PostHog (self-hosted)
```

**Why Better Auth over custom JWT?**
Better Auth is a comprehensive TypeScript-native auth library. Out of the box: email/password, Google OAuth, Apple Sign-In, JWT sessions, refresh tokens, rate limiting, account linking. Apple Sign-In is required by the App Store if you offer any social login. Building this custom is weeks of work and a security risk. Better Auth gives it in one package.

**Why Resend over SendGrid?**
Resend is built for developers. Simple API, React Email for templates, great deliverability, TypeScript-first, much simpler than SendGrid. Perfect for transactional emails (booking confirmations, badge earned, account verification).

**Why Ably over Socket.io?**
For a startup, managing a Socket.io server adds operational complexity. Ably is a managed WebSocket service with a generous free tier (200 concurrent connections free), SDKs for React Native and Node, and better reliability than self-hosted Socket.io. When you're ready to scale, it scales. When you don't need it, it costs nothing.

## 6.4 The Capture Endpoint (Single Most Important API Design)

One HTTP call does everything:

```typescript
POST /api/capture
Content-Type: multipart/form-data

Body:
  chapterId: string        // which chapter is being captured
  photo: File              // the moment photo
  lat: number              // GPS latitude
  lng: number              // GPS longitude
  note?: string            // optional one-liner (from the capture screen)
  isPublic?: boolean       // appear in community stories? default false

Server pipeline (synchronous, < 400ms response):
  1. Verify GPS within chapter radius (PostGIS ST_DWithin)
  2. Upload photo to Cloudflare R2 (Sharp resize: 1200px, WebP, 80 quality)
  3. Mark chapter complete (Drizzle update UserChapter)
  4. Award Serendipity Coins
  5. Return response immediately

Background jobs (BullMQ, async):
  6. Check badge conditions → award if met
  7. Create journal entry (always private, from this Capture)
  8. Create community post if isPublic=true
  9. Add passport stamp
  10. Check province completion → award if province done
  11. Check arc completion → trigger hidden gem reveal notification
  12. Update leaderboard (Redis sorted set)
  13. Send push notification summary: "You earned 80 coins + 1 badge"

Response (< 400ms):
{
  success: true,
  coinsEarned: 80,
  xpEarned: { tasteXP: 0, cultureXP: 50, actionXP: 0, wellnessXP: 0, adventureXP: 0 },
  badgeEarned: { name: "Food Connoisseur", image: "..." } | null,
  arcComplete: false,
  provinceComplete: "Central" | null
}
```

The client shows the celebration animation immediately from the response. Everything else processes in background.

# PART 11 — Reality Check

*The vision tells you where you're going. This section tells you why you might fail getting there. Read both together.*

---

## Q1: What is the ONE problem you solve on Day 1?

A traveller lands in Sri Lanka, opens Google Maps, sees 2,000 pins, and feels paralysed. They spend 45 minutes reading TripAdvisor reviews from 2018. They end up at the first thing on a listicle.

**The ONE job:** *"I'm here. Show me what to do right now that I will actually remember."*

Not planning. Not documenting. Not social. **Doing.**

The Capture Moment is the core action. Everything else — the map, the passport, the coins, the arcs — exists to lead someone to that one tap, or reward them after it. Protect this above everything.

---

## Q2: Why will a traveller open THIS app instead of Google Maps + Instagram?

Honest answer — three things those apps will never do:

**1. They tell you a place exists. We tell you what to actually do there.**
"Visit Sigiriya" is Google Maps. "Start climbing at 5:30am, stop at the frescoes before the crowds, the summit view lasts 40 minutes before haze" is SerendiGO.

**2. They don't help you not get ripped off.**
The Fair Price Guide is the single most useful feature for a first-time traveller in Sri Lanka. No other app does this specifically for Sri Lanka.

**3. Visiting earns something real.**
Going to Sigiriya on Google Maps earns you nothing. On SerendiGO it earns coins, a stamp, and an unlocked hidden gem. The doing becomes a story you collect.

**Honest caveat:** If the coins have no real redemption value and the marketplace is empty, advantages 2 and 3 collapse. Phase 1 must launch with at least 10 certified guides and the Fair Price Guide populated. Without that, there is no 10x advantage.

---

## Q3: What does "success in 7 days" look like for a user?

- Opened the app every morning to check the Today tab
- Completed at least 5 chapters across 2 arcs
- Experienced at least 1 Hidden Gem unlock — a place they never would have found otherwise
- Passport has at least 2 provinces with stamps
- Used the Fair Price Guide at least once

**The one proof of value:** They show the Passport to another traveller at a hostel and say *"look what I've collected"* — and that person downloads the app.

**The proof it's NOT working:** They opened it 3 times on day 1 and forgot about it.

---

## Q4: What is your true MVP — what are you NOT building?

**Cut for launch:**

| Feature | Why Cut |
|---------|---------|
| AI Trip Planner | Needs great content to draw from. Build content first. |
| Local Author Program | No reputation yet. Who will trust you to publish their story? |
| Community Fund | Needs revenue first. A fund with $0 is embarrassing. |
| Explorer+ Subscription | Don't gate features before people know the app has value. |
| Province Presence / Realtime | Needs user base. 2 people "in Central Province" is not a feature. |
| Offline Mode | Ship online. Fix offline when remote-area users report problems. |
| Community Feed | Private journal only for launch. Feed needs critical mass. |
| Flash Deals | Needs 20+ partner businesses. Day 1 you have zero. |
| Leaderboard | A leaderboard of 8 people is not motivating. |

**True MVP:**
The Island Map + Capture Moment + Passport + Today Tab + Fair Price Guide + 15 real arcs + 10 certified guides. That is it.

**If you can't name what you're cutting, you're still in idea mode.**

## Q5: How do you get your FIRST 100 users?

Not from the App Store.

**Channel 1 — Hostels (most important):** QR code cards at reception in 10 hostels across Colombo, Kandy, Ella, Galle. Offer to feature the hostel in nearby arcs. No money. Warm referrals from a trusted source.

**Channel 2 — Facebook groups:** "Sri Lanka Travel Guide", "Backpacking Sri Lanka". Post the Fair Price Guide as a useful graphic. Don't advertise. Be helpful first.

**Channel 3 — Reddit:** r/srilanka, r/backpacking, r/solotravel. Answer questions genuinely. Mention the app at the end, once.

**Channel 4 — Airport arrivals:** Stand at Bandaranaike International arrivals with a printed card for 3 weekends. Hand it to solo travellers. Uncomfortable. Highest conversion you will ever get.

**Why they trust a new app:** They don't trust the App Store listing. They trust it because a hostel they already trust recommended it, or another traveller showed them their Passport stamps in person. Trust transfers from people, not from app icons.

**Go physically.** Fly to Kandy. Find guides with 4.7+ TripAdvisor ratings. Meet them in person.

**The pitch:** "You're on Viator getting 70% of what you earn. I give you 88%. Take 3 bookings in your first month and ask each client to leave a review. If I don't send real bookings within 60 days, you lose nothing."

**Target 20 guides across 7 regions before launch:** Colombo, Kandy, Galle/South, Ella, Sigiriya/Cultural Triangle, Negombo, Jaffna.

Without supply, the marketplace feature is a lie.

## Q7: What happens when something goes wrong?

**Guide doesn't show up:** Full refund within 24 hours. Backup guide list per region. One warning per guide. Second no-show — delisted permanently.

**Tourist overcharged:** Fair Price Guide is advisory, not contractual. Marketplace bookings have locked prices — zero ambiguity. Be clear about this distinction everywhere.

**Unsafe situation:** Every booking confirmation shows emergency contacts (your number + 119 police + 1926 Tourist Police). Every guide is ID-verified. Build a one-tap "I need help" button that sends GPS location to a pre-set emergency contact. Recommend travel insurance at booking.

**Write the Terms of Service and Trust & Safety page before you launch. Not after your first incident.**

## Q8: Who creates and maintains the content?

**You. At the start. No shortcuts.**

The first 15 arcs are written by you, personally visited and verified. This takes 4–6 weeks in Sri Lanka. AI-generated content is obvious. Outsourced content without local knowledge is dangerous.

**After the foundation is set:**
You write → Local Authors submit (editorial review) → AI assists drafting (you edit) → Community flags outdated info

**Quality gate:** No arc ships without personal visit OR a verification call with a local who has been there in the last 30 days. Every "Before You Go" field shows a "Last verified" date. Content with 3+ community flags is hidden pending review.

**Maintenance is the hidden cost.** Entry fees change. Places close. Budget 4 hours per week just updating existing content. This never goes away.

## Q9: What will you measure weekly?

Three metrics. Not more.

**1. Captures per DAU** — Target: ≥ 2.0
Core loop health. If users open but don't capture, the discovery-to-action flow is broken.

**2. Day 7 Return Rate (trip-cohort adjusted)** — Target: ≥ 60%
Of users still in Sri Lanka on day 7, how many opened the app? Standard D7 doesn't work for travel — people physically leave.

**3. Guide Booking Conversion** — Target: ≥ 8% of guide profile views → booking request
Revenue health. If people look but don't book, there's a trust or friction problem.

**Never track:** Downloads. 10,000 downloads from a Reddit post means nothing. 40 users who captured every day for a week means everything.
